It's More than What Meets the Eye: Wedding Cake Structures

 

No Wedding is complete without a Wedding Cake. Wedding Cakes have evolved over the years and now appear in different shapes, sizes and styles.

Your Wedding Cake is not simply another part of your Wedding Day and procession. It is one of the many symbolic items that you share with your better half and your Guests. So, when choosing a Wedding Cake think through these few factors before finalising.

Your Wedding Theme:

Every wedding has a wedding theme even though you may not deliberately say it out loud. It may be something as simple as a colour theme. Your cake should match your overall wedding theme you have selected. For example, if your hosting a beach theme your wedding cake should be related to a beach theme either in colour, size and/ or in the components it incorporates such as sea shells.

Real Cake or Dummy Structure:

Your cake structure can be made completely of real cake or it could be a dummy cake structure with a single layer or portion made of real cake. Real cake structure are comparatively expensive but serves a dual purpose of been served as a desert item to a lunch or dinner menu. The downside of real wedding cakes is the height restriction. Unless it’s made innovatively or on a solid non edible structure the height of a wedding cake made of real cake can easily go up to 3 layers of a minimum of 2. Dummy structures come in two different varieties. The more conventional been one layer made of real cake for the couple to cut and eat. The other is a more recent approach where the cake cut and eaten by the couple is separately kept from the dummy cake structure. Dummy cake structures have no height restriction except on ability of the cake maker and the height of the hall from the floor to the ceiling.

                                   

Height of the Cake Structure:

The ideal height of a cake structure for a wedding should at least be 3 layers if made of real cake or a minimum of 5 layers if it is a dummy structure. Height matters since you don’t want the cake to look very insignificant amongst the rest of the hall decorations. Some choose to include flowers with their cake structure just so that the cake structure looks part of the same ceremony. You don’t want it look out of place, unless your Cake Structure is the main highlight of your Wedding.

There are still more factors couples could think about when finalising their Wedding Cake Structure.

Adding the couple’s monogram is another add-on you could consider. This gives the cake that subtle hint of it been made just for you.

You also have the option of going completely traditional and having kiribath and kavili. There is always the option of having both if you are having a poruwa ceremony.

The biggest decision couples have to make is to choose who makes their Cake Structure. Your budget, the cake maker’s style, can they do what you have in mind, are they available and free for delivery on your wedding day, their approachability and flexibility are a few important things you need to check before finalising your cake supplier.
