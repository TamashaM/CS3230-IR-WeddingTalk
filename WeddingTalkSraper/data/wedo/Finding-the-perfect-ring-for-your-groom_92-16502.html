Finding the perfect ring for your groom

May 09, 2018 10:52 am

The wedding ring is one of the most important elements of a wedding as it represents the love and commitment between the bride and the groom.
 
As the wedding day approaches, it is no secret about the challenges brides have to face when hunting for that perfect ring for your soulmate.
 
The wedding ring will be on one of the most significant piece of jewellery a man will own in his lifetime.
 
While finding the ideal ring seems simple but it can be pretty overwhelming when you realize the different options you see on the market; let’s make things easy for you with a few pointers you need to think about before settling for his ring.
 
Think about the budget
Planning a wedding doesn’t come cheap so it’s really important to work on your budget when it comes to choosing a ring for your would-be husband.
 
You can choose to buy your wedding rings in a pair with similar designs or opt for different choices according to your preference. 
 
Correct ring size
After you’ve decided on how you are willing to spend on his ring, it is time to find his ring size. Determining the ring size can save you from heartbreak when you finally find a ring but realize that it wouldn’t fit him.
 
While the cold makes your fingers slimmer, it is important to measure the ring size at a mild temperature so that you don’t end up with a ring that’s too small for him!
 
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
A durable type of Metal
Choosing the type of metal you’d like for his ring is essential as it will influence the durability, cost and maintenance of the ring.
 
It is important to ensure that your future husband isn’t allergic to the type of metal you select. It is a good idea to sit with him and discuss on the following choice of metals:
 
  • Platinum 
The rarest of all available precious metals yet it is also the strongest, whitest and most durable.
 
Platinum offers excellent resistance to corrosion and has a hypoallergenic nature and that is why it is also known as one of the most expensive metals!
            Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
  • Palladium
Palladium is extremely popular amongst grooms. It delivers a classy, dark grey shine that’s hard to mimic from other metals.
 
Palladium contains similar properties like platinum as it is a part of the platinum family but much cheaper!
Image Courtesy: www.starweddingbands.com
 
  • White Gold
White gold is made from a mix of yellow gold and white metal to deliver a shine comparable to platinum.
In order to maintain the white shine, the white gold wedding ring will need to be plated with rhodium every few years.
 
  • Yellow Gold
Yellow gold is the most traditional of all wedding band metals. The metal is timeless and has played a major role in cultural and religious ceremonies for centuries due to its precious nature and rarity. 
Image Courtesy: Pinterest 
 
  • Rose Gold
Rose gold is one of the trendiest and most popular metals at the moment with brides and grooms alike, making for a beautiful set of matching couple wedding rings. 
Image Courtesy: DHgate
 
  • Silver
Silver is the most common of all precious metals, easier to source and much cheaper to buy too. If you are unable to make up your mind, a silver ring is perfect as a “stand-in” ring till you decide. 
Image Courtesy: Bling Jewellery
 
Wedding Band Profile
There is a variety of classic ring profiles to choose from, this is one of the most important factors as every other aspect mentioned above. Finding the perfect ring profile ensures maximum comfort for daily wear.
 
- Classic Court Wedding Band
This is one of most common shapes as it features rounded interiors and exteriors, giving the visual of a nearly rounded ring. Due to this, it is one of the most comfortable rings to wear.
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
- D-Shaped Wedding Ring
Similar to the classic court profile, a D-shaped wedding band is the second most popular. If you want something traditional but with a better fitting, this band is highly recommended as it possesses a flat interior and a rounded exterior like the letter “D”.
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
- Flat Rings
If you prefer something more sophisticated with a cleaner finish, then this modern design is perfect for your soon-to-be husband. The flat court ring offers a flat exterior and a comfortable rounded interior making it very popular amongst men right now.
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
- Concave
The concave profile showcases a curved outer surface and has a much more elegant interior just like the interior of the classic court profile. The concave profile guarantees to give you a beautiful and comfortable ring.
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
Style
While most men’s wedding rings are being set with brilliant diamonds and other precious gemstones, it really is up to you. Whether you want a plain, patterned, polished or matte ring, the choice is absolutely yours. Always remember that the wedding band must reflect his personality and you and your future husband as a couple.
