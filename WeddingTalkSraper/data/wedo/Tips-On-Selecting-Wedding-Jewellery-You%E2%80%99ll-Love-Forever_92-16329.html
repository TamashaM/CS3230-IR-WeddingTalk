Tips On Selecting Wedding Jewellery You’ll Love Forever

November 07, 2017 12:27 pm

Jewellery has always been a key part of any bride’s ensemble, having the power to complete and enhance the entire wedding look or, if chosen inappropriately, look unflattering and be destined to remain in a sealed box after the big day.

It is, therefore, no surprise that most brides devote a significant amount of time and thought to pick out their wedding jewellery. Usually, a sizeable expense in the wedding budget, the purchase of jewellery is a considerable investment and, as such, deserves quite some effort when being chosen.

So here are some useful tips to help pick out the perfect jewellery to make any bride shine on her wedding day; something she will love to wear repeatedly throughout her life.


Look around, choose a trusted jeweller 

Sri Lanka has a considerable number of reputed, longstanding, reliable jewellers, most of whom offer a fine level of craftsmanship. Do pay attention to the purity of metals and authenticity of precious stones used.

Leave yourself enough time to visit at least a couple of other jewellers to compare designs, quality and price range so you are not rushed into a decision you might later regret.


Don’t be overwhelmed 

Image courtesy: nithyakalyani jewellers

When tray after tray of shiny gold trinkets and necklaces dripping in precious stones are being paraded in front of you under gleaming spotlights, it can be hard to keep track of what you wanted in the first place!

While it’s good to keep an open mind, it’s also important to remain focused on your particular design and functional needs, while also sticking to your pre-planned budget but allowing yourself a leeway of around 10% if you see the perfect piece you absolutely love.


Mix tradition with your personal style 


Image courtesy: nithyakalyani jewellers

Traditional jewellery designs incorporate culture and elegance into your wedding celebration and indeed, are typically pieces you can bring out for any special occasion.

While you may feel duty bound to take the traditional route when it comes to wedding jewellery, don’t forego your individual preferences completely in favour of custom. Do try to find pieces that reflect your personality, as you will most likely find yourself reaching for these accessories when dressing up for a celebration.

In terms of metals, while gold is seen as the traditional favourite for weddings, many young couples are increasingly choosing two-toned (a mix of yellow and white gold) rings for their wedding ceremony. Also feel free to personalise your wedding bands with inscriptions or other engravings, or even design it yourself for that ultimate personal touch.


Image source: pinterest.com

 

Choose jewellery you can see yourself wearing again 

While your wedding dress might be retired to the closet after the big day, your marital gems should be something you keep wearing. Picking a classic design that can still be unique will ensure you have pieces that will endure ever-changing fashion trends and remain relevant.

Chunky, colourful stones, not your thing? Opt for understated elegance with simple diamond or pearl accessories. A good tip is to pick versatile, standout pieces that can be worn in numerous ways, or stand out on their own without being sported only as a set and can be worn during the day or at night.

 

Mix old with new 

Not every piece of jewellery you wear on your wedding day needs to be brand new. Wearing one or more pieces of heirloom or pre-owned jewellery will add a lovely touch of personal significance for you and your family.

What’s more, you’re guaranteed to treasure them throughout your life and safeguard to pass on to the next generation.


Take care of your bling 

Image source: phillymag.com​

Aftercare for your jewellery is an oft-forgotten aspect of maintaining quality and appearance. After your big day, don’t simply chuck your jewellery in a box and confine it to the back of your safety locker.

A simple wipe down with a soft cloth will help remove any traces of makeup and oils from the wedding function itself. Periodically, you can take your jewellery to be professionally cleaned and polished at most large reputed jewellers to restore its original brilliance and shine.
