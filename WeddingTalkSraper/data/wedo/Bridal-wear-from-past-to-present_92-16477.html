Bridal wear from past to present

February 01, 2018 3:06 pm

When the word “wedding” is mentioned, one of the first things to pop up in your mind is the wedding gown. The wedding dress is one of the most unforgettable attire a woman will ever wear. The gown symbolises the bride’s personality. 
 
Speaking to Sandani Perera, one of the upcoming bridal designers in town who exclusively caters to modern yet traditional bridal wear under her brand name, Bridal by Sandani Perera; tells me about all the ways bridal attire has transformed over the years and how special each decade was.
 
One thing that has remained the same is the classic white colour as it signifies purity of the soul. 
 
 
1950’s
The 1950s was a decade defined by modesty, elegance and etiquette. As the World War II had just ended, ultra-feminine styles and French lace was the “in” thing in this era.
 
Ballerina length dresses and gloves were a standard fashion while lacy tiers and bouncy frills dictated the wedding dress skirts. The 1950s also introduced the sweetheart neckline worn by popular actress, Grace Kelly on her wedding in 1956.
Image Source: Pinterest
 
1960’s
This decade was all about experimentation. Metallic embellishments and floral details on the dress were very trendy. Empire line silhouettes with high waistlines proved to be a popular choice in this era.
 
While necklines remained decent, veils tended to be shorter in length. Smock tops with puffy sleeves and Peter Pan type collars became popular later in this decade. 
Image Source: Pinterest
 
1970’s
Bohemian styles were much in fashion with longer sleeves flowing towards the forearm. Necklines were mostly square in shape and higher as popularised by Princess Anne during her wedding in 1973.
 
Many gowns featured a “dust ruffle”, a ruffle that surrounds the hem about 12 inches around it. Sandani tells me she loves the bridal styles of this era.
Image Source: Pinterest                 
 
1980’s
Cathedral trains, full-length veils and lace edged frills dominated this decade, says Sandani. Oversized shoulders were all the rage along with high mandarin collared necklines.
 
This era also witnessed the wedding of Princess Diana in 198; her unique style of puffy sleeves along with a dramatic train which was 24 feet long set the standard for the “fantasy wedding dress.”
Image Source: (Left) Pinterest (Right) Daily Mail
 
1990’s
The bridal couture took a drastic turn as brides opted for a more simple and minimal dress while focusing more on big hairstyles.
 
As embellishments such as lace and beading were being used rarely; flower crowns and straightforward simple veils were a growing trend in this era. Famous bridal designer Vera Wang became a household name during this period.
Image Source: Pinterest.com
 
2000’s
With more wedding ceremonies taking place in hotels and outdoor locations, this was the time when strapless gowns gained popularity. Brides started styling their hair with flowers and tiaras, while veils were no longer the custom.
 
Wedding style became more about personal style rather than following religious traditions. “Boho-chic” became the latest fashion as it influenced bridal wear with flowing styles and lace detailing.
Image Source: Pinterest.com
 
2010’s
An era that we are all familiar with, brides tend to fancy curve-hugging dresses while the mermaid silhouettes are proven to be one of the most popular choices, says Sandani.
 
Ever since the entire world watched the wedding of Kate Middleton in 2011, the long-sleeved gown and full romantic veil that is draped over the face gained a lot of popularity and was copied by many.
 
Today, textured details, complex beading and crystals are some of the common features on the modern day bride.
Image Source: pinterest.com
