Flattering and fashionable outfits for mother-of-the-bride

February 07, 2018 1:06 pm

For any mother of the bride (MOB), what they wear at their daughter’s wedding will probably be the second most important outfit they will choose, after their own wedding of course.

There will be as many eyes on you, as the bride’s mother, on the big day, plus don’t forget all those pictures!

Regardless of what style of ensemble you end up choosing to wear, something to keep in mind is that your outfit should ideally complement the colour palette of the retinue and coordinate with the wedding theme, although this is not always the rule.

In addition, make sure your outfit is properly tailored and well-fitting; nothing will ruin your look faster than an ill-fitting sari jacket or dress.

Here are a few outfit suggestions to help you choose the mother of all outfits and look fabulous as you watch your daughters get hitched!

 

A traditional sari for unparalleled elegance 

A sari is the foremost choice amongst most MOBs, and quite rightly so. Nothing conveys a more dignified look than six yards of fabric, draped beautifully and pinned neatly into place.

Image source: Pothys.com

The choice of sari can vary depending on cultural background, as well as location and time of the wedding ceremony. Be it a traditional vibrant kanjeevaram, a dignified Kandyan sari with accompanying long beaded necklaces or an elegant georgette number, MOBs, look no further than the sari for a look guaranteed to bring you compliments on the big day!


Try a new twist on the conventional sari designs

If you’re keen to add some pizazz to your wedding day ensemble, but still prefer a sari look, there are plenty of variations you can try out (think lace, sequins, brocade!).

Long sleeved sari blouses and jackets with intricate beading and embroidery are very much in vogue and are perfect for all you MOBs who love to dress with flair.

This look is best accompanied by an appropriate sari draping style to show off your attractive blouses.


Evening gowns are a sophisticated choice

While no mother would want to upstage her daughter on her wedding day, there is no reason you should choose a dress design that is matronly or frumpy.

If an evening gown or dress is your choice of outfit, think elegant and tailored when deciding on the exact design – a fitted bodice with a flared skirt looks great on most women.

Image source: (Left) nuethical.com (right) simpledresses.com 

If you want to show off your toned arms, go with a sleeveless sheath dress, or a belted gown to showcase your trim waistline. With the plethora of appealing colours you will undoubtedly encounter, you might want to avoid wearing white or ivory or anything too similar in colour or style to the bride’s wedding outfit.

Metallics, jewel tones, or even pastels are all choices that can make you look stunning – pick your colour keeping your skin tone in mind.


Traditional Indian shalwar kameez for a graceful and classy look

The occasion of your daughter’s wedding means you’re guaranteed to be on your toes for hours. For a no-fuss, fairly comfortable, yet tasteful look, you might find a shalwar kameez more to your liking.

There is no end to the variety of styles you can find among this genre of clothing – be it anarkali's, kaftan-style or straight cut, just to mention a few. Choose rich fabrics like silk, brocade or chiffon to really stand out.

If you prefer a more simple shalwar kameez, do pick a dupatta or shawl that is heavily embellished to get your share of the limelight.


Accessorise! accessorise!

Your accessories can elevate your ensemble to Instagram-worthiness. Don’t be afraid to use bold jewellery to liven up a minimal outfit – think bangles with intricate designs, glittering necklaces or sparkling earrings, all meant to complement your ensemble of course.

Add a vibrant coloured or beaded clutch to complete your wedding look. For an extra glamorous touch, choose an elegant hair accessory, or go the more subtle route with beautiful fresh flowers tucked into your up-do.
