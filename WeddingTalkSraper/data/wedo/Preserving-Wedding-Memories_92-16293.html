Preserving Wedding Memories

March 28, 2017 3:26 pm

A wedding is so much more than just one day.  It is a season in your life.  Your wedding is a loving personal project which you pour your heart and soul into. It is a moment intended to be never repeated and a day you would like to revisit in your mind a thousand times in time to come. So for posterity’s Sake or otherwise there are many reasons why you should and could preserve the beautiful memories and moments from your wedding. Here are some ideas.


The Wedding Planner

Every girl devises their own method and system of dealing with the multitude of tasks and chores that go into organizing a fabulous wedding. For most it might be an excel sheet with to do lists and budgets or a folder in their computer or tab with tons of reference images and wedding ideas downloaded from the net. The good old fashioned girl’s note book might also be worthwhile considering. Purchase a pretty one the moment you know a wedding is on the cards. Use it to write down ideas, contact numbers and appointments with wedding vendors and to do lists. You’ll be surprised how quickly the pages fill up with scribbled notes, pasted fabric, paper samples and cash receipts. You can flip through the pages of this notebook 25 years from now and be surprised as to how much of the little details from this time in your life you had in fact forgotten.

 

 

Your Bridal Wear

Your bridal attire may it be a dress or a saree is the most prized souvenir from your wedding day. Emotionally attached to it and unable to part with it, the bride usually relegates the bridal attire to a  corner in the bride’s closet straight after the wedding. However there are so many creative ways to upcycle it. Consider using the fabric from the dress or saree to create a robe for your child’s baptism or a dress for your daughter’s first birthday. The fabric from the bridal could also be turned into throw pillows for your living room, a decorative quilt for your bedroom, a decorative runner for a table or even a soft toy for your first born. A sample of your wedding lace will look beautiful when framed and hung in your bedroom. Lace and beads picked from your outfit will also look lovely turned into an artisan’s pendant or bracelet. There are countless ways in which you can bring your wedding outfit out from the closet and into your day to day life.


Scrapbooking

 

Source: scrapbook.com


Scrap booking the wedding journey is a great way to preserve memories. Scrapbooking itself is a form of art. A quick google search will give you a sea of ideas on how exactly to engage in creative scrapbooking. But the basic idea is to:


( a ) Purchase a scrap book.


(b ) Collect a wide variety of memorabilia and keepsakes from your wedding - You will of course need basic items such as photographs from your wedding, wedding stationery, menu cards etc as well as some unique and interesting items such as receipts, bills and invoices from wedding vendor/supplier invoices, wedding gift cards, perhaps the label from the bottle of champagne used for the wedding toast, the crumpled up piece of paper with the groom’s speech written on it, a ribbon from your cake box, dried flower petals from your bouquet etc. The more novel and quirky the items the better!


( c ) Collect some scrapbooking embellishments - The internet is filled with sites where you can purchase commercially prepared embellishments for scrapbooking such as stickers, glitter, craft paper , ribbons and beads etc.  The idea is to gather craft supplies of your choice which you can use to decorate the pages of your scrapbook as you paste you memorabilia on it.


( d ) Piece it all together – Dedicate a quiet day to sit and paste your mementos on the pages of the scrap book and decorate the pages. Don’t forget to write little comments and notes to on the pages as you go along.
The end product is a beautiful scrap book of memories which you can indulge in many times over in the years to come.


Cake For Your First Anniversary

If your wedding cake structure is all cake and not rigifoam, there is a very sweet 1st anniversary tradition you could partake in. The top tier of your wedding cake (complete with the cake topper) can be wrapped up and preserved in your deep freezer. When it is time to celebrate your first anniversary this can be defrosted and consumed with your better half, family and friends. Make it a little celebration and pair it with leftover wine or champagne from your wedding bottle stock. This will undoubtedly be a sweet reminder of your wedding day.


Marriage Advice Cards

Source: paperandlace.com


Though usually a tradition at pre wedding bridal showers, filling out marriage advice cards can also be a fun activity for your guests on the day of your wedding. There will be so many different folks at your wedding and each one will have their own spin on what takes to build a good marriage. So what you end up with is a beautiful mix of sentiments penned especially for you two. Give the cards out to guests with sealable envelopes so that their advice remains private till you open them on a later date of your choice to read and reminisce.


The Wedding Cards


Especially in Sri Lanka, every wedding gift comes with a beautiful handwritten wedding card from one of  your special guests. The sentimental bride, usually collects these in a bundle straight after the big post-wedding unwrapping of the gifts,  hoping to hang on to them as keepsakes from the wedding. However in most cases before the 1st anniversary these do make its way to the garbage bin after it has done the rounds sitting on various surfaces in the house gathering dust and dirt. So what is the best way to preserve your wedding cards. Simply bunch them up together (preferably in some sensible order based on color or size) and get it wirebound into a little booklet. If you already have a keepsake collection box for your wedding filled with knick knacks, this wedding cards collection booklet can go right into that box !


The Good Old Photo Album


A good old photo album and framed wedding portraits is a must for any couple and it is still the most formal preservation of wedding memories in use. However wedding photography has evolved into so many exciting styles that you are likely to stumble upon a photo artist who takes pictures that you really like.  Take time to research and find that kind of a photographer so that the photographs in your album really capture the mood of the day and your own personal vibe as a couple. Pre wedding shoots are also a great way to document some extra memories from those wedding days so give some thought into giving that a go too.


All being said the best way to preserve the magic of your wedding is to stay in love with your partner. Consider that too!
