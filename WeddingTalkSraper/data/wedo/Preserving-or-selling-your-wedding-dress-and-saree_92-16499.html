Preserving or selling your wedding dress and saree

April 27, 2018 12:26 pm

The vows have been uttered, the cake cut, a few laughs and a few tears – the wedding is over. And now you are left with this magical white dress or the grand embroidered saree, an outfit you may never wear again but feel vastly attached to, you need to deal with it some way.

Whether you decide to preserve your dress, sell it or donate it, it needs to be cleaned and stored in a proper way. 

Image Courtesy: Pinterest

Preservation

If you have decided to preserve your wedding dress or your saree, then make sure to get a professional cleaning done. This is the first step when it comes to wedding dress preservation.

Always remember, the sooner you can get your dress to the dry cleaners, the better it is.

If you think it’s alright to wait a bit and leisurely give your dress to the cleaners, then you are making a mistake as stains will or would have set in.

If you are off on your honeymoon and unable to do it yourself, arrange for a bridesmaid or your mother to take it for you. 

 

Once your dress has been cleaned properly, it is time to store it!

Protect your dress from direct sunlight, which can quickly turn your dress or saree yellow. Never ever store your dress in regular plastic zip lock kind off bags as plastic holds moisture and can possibly discolour your dress.

Instead, opt for a professional wedding box or a wedding chest. 

DIY Preservation

As professional wedding dress preservers aren’t available in Sri Lanka as yet, there are some ways you can preserve your wedding dress yourself. Since you don’t need anything too fancy for this method, a gentle laundry detergent will do the trick!

Remember to always use white cotton gloves when handling the wedding dress.
Use soft bristled brushes to clean out the stains paying extra attention to the hem and underarms, as hard scrubbing could damage the fabric and the delicate work. 

Do not bleach your dress or saree.

The delicate fabric can be irreversibly damaged if you use this harsh chemical. 


Image Courtesy: weddingbee.com

Once your wedding dress is clean and dry, wrap it in acid-free tissue paper or pre-washed unbleached muslin cloth. Avoid using coloured tissue papers as it can stain your dress over time.

Place layers of tissue paper or muslin between the folds the dress to avoid any kind of permanent creasing. Stuff the bust of the dress and sleeves if there are with tissue paper to uphold the full shape of the dress.


 Image Courtesy: Pinterest

Finally, place your dress or saree in an acid-free plastic garment bag and store it in a dark and cool place. If you choose to hang your wedding dress, then remember to use a padded hanger.

By having your wedding dress hanged, good air circulation is offered and your dress has a less risk of being permanently creased.

It is better to put in a few silica packets along to control the humidity. 

Do not store your wedding dress or saree in a garage or a basement as humidity and temperatures in these places are not suitable for preservation purposes. 

It is always safe to check your wedding dress once every two to three years and refold it to ensure no permanent creasing occurs.

Selling

Not everyone wants to go through the hassle of preserving, rather they feel donating or selling as a better option. If you do decide to sell it, there are a few general rules. A used gown that’s in great condition and less than two years can sell for at least 50% of the retail price. You can maximize the value of your dress by having it professionally cleaned.

Picking out a wedding dress is a personal choice depending on your style; so what you want to do with it afterwards is totally upto an individual bride.

If you are a sentimental type like me, the kind who needs all the physical memories of the big day, then it’s definitely better to preserve it safely.

If you are the type who needs more closet space and feel that you want to recoup the money and put it towards a new home, honeymoon or baby preparations, then it’s better to sell it.

The choice is entirely yours!
