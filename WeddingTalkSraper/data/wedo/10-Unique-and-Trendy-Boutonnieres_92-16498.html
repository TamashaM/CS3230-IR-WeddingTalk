10 Unique and Trendy Boutonnieres

April 26, 2018 4:05 pm

When the groom walks into the wedding ceremony, all eyes are on him just like everyone waits to catch a glimpse of the bride in her stunning dress.
 
One thing we notice about the groom and his groomsmen is the little flower placed on their left side right above the heart.
 
Ever wonder what exactly that little flower is called?
 
It is known as a boutonniere.
 
Just as the flowers of the bride and her bridesmaids are important, a boutonniere is equally significant.
 
Traditionally, a groom wears a flower from the bride’s bouquet to symbolize his ties to the bride. Though boutonnieres are small, they definitely make a big statement.
 
They add a punch of personality, colour and texture to the groom and groomsmen’s outfit. 
 
While flowers are the customary choice for boutonnieres, guys these days can be found wearing different types of boutonnieres that are meaningful to the couple-to-be.
 
Let’s have a look at the trendy ways you can wear a boutonniere that looks great, coordinate with the bridal blooms as well as feel masculine too:
 
#1 Love Note
The main attraction is this groom’s boutonniere – beyond the colourful ranunculus flowers and carnations; is a sweet card, written with a simple promise: “Forever.”
Image Courtesy: Amilia Photography
 
#2 Tribute to your ancestors
Incorporating a family heirloom like a beautiful brooch on the wedding day is a sweet and meaningful tradition that holds high sentimental value as well adds a personal touch to the wedding suit.
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
#3 Edible Boutonniere
If the groom is an expert in the kitchen, then pay respect to his culinary skills with an entirely edible boutonniere, like this one fashioned from a baby carrot and fresh herbs like rosemary and lavender.
 
This style not only is unique but also shows his special capabilities. If you are having a destination wedding in the tropics, then this mini edible pineapple is perfect for you!
 
Image Courtesy: (Left) Zev Fisher (Right) Sweet Tea Photography 
 
#4 Long Lasting Boutonniere 
For a wedding accessory that will last long after the celebration, opt for a floral boutonniere fashioned from colourful felt.
 
This also helps you coordinate colours that match the bride’s bouquet and décor.
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
#5 Laser Cut Boutonnieres
Give a unique touch to the traditional boutonnieres by these laser cut ones. Laser cut boutonnieres for the groom and his groomsmen can be nicknames, wedding roles and inside jokes.
 
I totally love this one!
 
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
#6 Super Hero Legos
Little superhero Lego figurines are small enough not be distracting but speak volumes to the groom’s personality. This is also a great way to infuse personalization into the ceremony while giving off a unique and fun look.
 
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
#7 Vineyard details
You’ve probably heard of wine bottle decorations; now take it up a notch with these boutonnieres. The combination of coral and corks is ideal for a charming rustic themed wedding. 
Image Courtesy: Ruffled Roses
 
#8 Nautical Knot
No flowers? No problem! Instead, incorporate a notable detail specific to your wedding theme into the boutonniere.
A nautical knot is essential for a preppy summer ceremony most likely at a beach, and it also complements this groom’s navy striped tie so perfectly. 
 
#9 Paper Flower Boutonniere
Do you and your partner have a book or a passage that’s especially momentous to both of you? Turn it into a boutonniere by photocopying or printing out the text and make them into floral shapes making your day extra romantic.
Image Courtesy: Pinterest
 
#10 Metallic Accents
Adding just a hint of gold turns a regular boutonniere into a one-of-a-kind accessory. Spray paint heartier floral elements like leaves and seeds, for a pop of metallic giving his suit “the” extra bling.
 
It’s only fair that your groom looks every bit as stylish as you do, and a great way to make sure he stands out is with a fashionable boutonniere!
