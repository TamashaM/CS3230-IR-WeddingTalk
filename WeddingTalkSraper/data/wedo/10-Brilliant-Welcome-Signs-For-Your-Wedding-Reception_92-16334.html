10 Brilliant Welcome Signs For Your Wedding Reception

November 09, 2017 11:50 am

First impressions are very important. People judge the rest of their journey based on that. Your welcome sign is the first impression of your wedding reception, therefore your guests will make assumptions depending on that.

Why not make it a good one?  

Here's a list of some of the uniquely eye-catching signs that you can incorporate into your wedding day! 

10.  LED light box
 
Image source: heavy.com

A simple yet eye-catching welcome sign for the guests. Put up creative wordings on it, they’ll shine through the bright lighting. Who doesn’t like a neon look? It can be used for other purposes once the wedding is over; you can change the words accordingly. 

 

09. Activity signs 

Image source: (Left) idecor4youstore.com (Right) pinterest.com

Arrows pointed in different directions, indicating where the different events of the day are taking place. It could be an entertaining thing for the guests. Get creative by making them at home, a good way to avoid extra costs. 

 

08. Billboard announcement 

Image source: etsy.com

Put up a huge billboard, making it look like a scene from Hollywood. Definitely something the guests will remember.

I mean, how many weddings do you come across with a billboard as their welcome sign? Could be a great way to take memorable pictures at your reception. 

 

07. Key Board

Image source: pinterest.com

Basically, this is a board with hooks that can hold car keys. Your guests will definitely get a laugh out of it. This could be something you do instead of having valets; you can also cut down on costs. Get innovative and personalise it to your tastes.

 

06. Wooden drink board 

It’s a wooden board with words on them, anything related to cocktails. When your guests enter, this board can be put up at the entrance, where they have to choose two words from the board.

According to the words of their choice, the guest will be handed a cocktail. Who wouldn’t be happy about starting off at a party with booze? Have a mocktail list as well for the juniors invited to your wedding.

 

05. A waving flag

Image source: pinterest.com 

The couple's symbol can be etched on the flag and put up on a flagpole at the entrance. It could be a little bit of a risk, with a Sri Lankan crowd, but if you’re the type of couple that doesn’t mind a bit of that, then go ahead do it. At least, you’ll be the talk, even if they don’t like it.

 

04. Wooden calligraphy sign 

If you’re going for something minimal yet elegant, your search ends here. Wood and calligraphy; two things that can never be overrated. Capture a cool rustic look at your wedding reception. It can even be something more of a DIY.

 

03. Retro vintage sign

This is for couples who are not fond of floral or any form of designs and want to go for something more simple. Choose two sophisticated colours (something more on the darker side), one for your letter and the other for your background.

Spell out your welcome sign on a board; the size would be up to the couple, but a minimum of 24x24 is required. 
 
02. Detailed signage

Image source: (Left) pinterest.com (Right) notonthehighstreet.com

This could be a sign holding the day’s details, for example; your date, day, time, coordinates. Go ahead, be original and turn it into your own masterpiece. Maybe even match your reception theme to your welcome sign, a nautical theme for the party.

You don’t see a lot of those at Sri Lankan weddings, so again if you’re trying to do something different, this is it.

 

01. Homemade glass frame

Image source: Pinterest.com

A homemade glass frame with calligraphy on it, a graceful yet fashionable way to welcome your guests. This won’t make it to the talk, but it is definitely a safe and sure way to rock that wedding sign. This is definitely for you, if you’re going for something basic for the party. 

Choose any welcome sign mentioned above based on your tastes and theme, you’re sure to rock it despite how different they all are. 

Happy wedding!
