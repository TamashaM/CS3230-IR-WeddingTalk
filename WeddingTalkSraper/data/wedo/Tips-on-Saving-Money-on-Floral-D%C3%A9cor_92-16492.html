Tips on Saving Money on Floral Décor

April 17, 2018 2:40 pm

Spending a large amount of money on floral décor at a wedding can almost be termed a guilty pleasure.

Most brides are torn between feeling that it may be a rather wasteful investment also knowing that flowers are an essential element of a beautiful wedding and that nothing creates an air of romance quite like flowers do.

For concerned couples, here are some tips on striking a balance and saving some money on floral décor. 

The oldest trick in the book is to use artificial flowers in places where guests are most unlikely to notice. This would ideally be areas in your setting which guests do not come into close contact with or areas where they capture only a fleeting glance of the decor.

For example décor at the entrance of the hall, settee backs and cake table décor can feature artificial flowers and table centrepieces which the guests marvel at could feature natural flowers.

You could also opt for an equal mix of artificial and fresh flowers if your florist is very skilled at blending the two together inconspicuously.

Almost all florists offer this option for budget concerned brides so an exploring of options is always worthwhile.

 

You could consider an outdoor garden setting where smaller centerpieces and elaborate floral décor are not usually called for.

A garden wedding venue can be easily dressed up to achieve a dreamy look with soft lighting, lanterns and fairy lights etc. without using excessive amounts of flowers.   

Image source : weddingforward.com

There are some very creative floral designers who create décor themes that involve considerable amounts of non-floral décor items.

For example, the theme could include small flower pots used in conjunction with candelabra, candles, tea lights and reflective mirrors.

You could opt for a foliage based theme which includes exotic plants for a fresh and minimalistic look. A more exotic option could be to feature table centerpieces with fruits and berries.

Image source: (Left) southboundbride.com (Right) knotsvilla.com

Even decorative ornaments and props can be used to create unconventional centerpieces. Imagine centerpieces with coloured feathers, spray painted metallic tree branches or foliage, lanterns, seashells, decanters of coloured water etc.

In fact there are event designers who specialise in non-floral wedding décor who you could engage with for the purpose. 

Make arrangements to re-purpose floral décor items on your wedding day. For example, floral décor from a church wedding can be brought back to the reception venue to be used as reception décor.

Bridesmaid bouquets can go in vases in the head table as flower arrangements as they sit down to dine. A little bit of coordination and manpower on the day of the wedding is all you need to make this happen. 

Image source: weddingforward.com

For evening weddings held in indoor venues, you can minimise on your floral décor and use lighting effects to create a glamorous look. You could use colour wash lighting to give the entire venue a colour hue of your choice which is absolutely magical in the night.

Rotating scanners and laser lights can splash about coloured lighting to give a bit more of a dynamic feel and to make the reception venue come alive. You could use spotlights on the dance floor, cake table and each guest table and even use light projections of designs, logos, motifs etc on the reception ceilings and walls to create a special look.

The walls of the receptions can be covered with drapes and fairy lights and you can be assured that the floral décor you do have in place will be upstaged by the dazzling beauty of such a backdrop.

What’s more, most wedding reception venues already come with inbuilt lighting systems which means that you will not need to spend extra money to make use of it. 

When contemplating where to compromise with regard to floral décor, it helps to list down the specific types of floral décor in its order of importance so you know where you could cut costs and save money.

All weddings would need table centerpieces for each guest table and these are difficult to do away with.

Table centerpieces can be as elaborate or simple as you require them to be and it is also important to bear in mind that the cost relating to table centerpieces will increase in proportion to the number of guests you hope to invite.

Thereafter it is important to consider some essential items such the poruwa or a decorated throne which are must-haves for religious and cultural reasons relating to marriage ceremonies.

Then there are the optional pieces of décor such as the settee backs, head table décor and entrance décor.

Finally, all of these different elements should be thought of in context of your specific reception venue in order to understand how important each element is within the overall look you like to create. 

It should also be noted that with so many creative wedding florists in Sri Lanka it is possible to create a magical ambiance to suit any budget with or without flowers.

What is important is that you are upfront with your event designer and floral décor vendor regarding your budget for floral décor, which in turn will challenge them to work out and deliver a décor solution to fit your requirements.
