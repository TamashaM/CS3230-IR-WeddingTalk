Do’s and Dont's of Honeymoon Planning

July 31, 2017 12:54 pm

After the craziness of the wedding, the honeymoon is when you can finally unwind, rejuvenate (even if it's an adventure based honeymoon), and enjoy the company of your spouse. So to make sure the honeymoon will be the experience you want it to be, consider this list of planning do’s and dont’s.

Do’s

 

Do ask and you shall receive

When making reservations, do inform them that you are on your honeymoon. You will be surprised at the special packages, upgrades and freebies you will be entitled to as honeymooners. Also take this opportunity to let the hotel know of any other requirements and of allergies you may have.

Do consider a guest sourced honeymoon

Weddings are expensive affairs that will leave a significant dent in your wallet. So why not allow your guests to pitch-in in place of traditional gifts? This is often a great option for guests who can’t figure out what to get you; and contributing towards a holiday that will result in wonderful lifelong memories for you will definitely appeal to them.

You can do this by setting up a wishing well and inform your guests that their contributions will go towards paying for your honeymoon. While this kind of thing is still novel in the country, it is definitely picking up steam as more and more couples weigh the high cost of their wedding against planning an equally extravagant honeymoon.

 

Do pay heed to travel times

 

Thankfully, Sri Lanka is situated close to a lot of great honeymoon destinations (Maldives, Bali), but if you’re hoping to venture further than that, then it is important that you consider how much time you will spend travelling vs how long you will be at your honeymoon destination.

If travel time is very long, complete with equally long layovers and you’re only at your destination for one week, you may spend a significant chunk of your honeymoon jetlagged.

Do book any activities beforehand

 

If you have plans to enjoy couples massages at the hotel’s spa, or any guided tours or hot air balloon rides, book ahead so you won’t be left disappointed when these activities are overbooked.

Do check the weather

This is especially important if you will be spending a lot of time outdoors. Googling the weather ahead of time ensures you won’t have to deal with any unexpected surprises such as showers during what you wrongly assumed was dry weather. Seems obvious, but it happens.

 

Dont’s

 

Don’t whisk her off immediately after your wedding

 

 

Sure, it may seem romantic to head straight for your honeymoon the second you’re done with the wedding. The cons of doing this are quite high though - you could forget things such as your boarding passes, you may have close shaves with making it to the terminal in time, and you’ll most definitely be exhilarated but exhausted.

The smartest thing is to wait a day or two so you have enough time to wind down and be relaxed when leaving for your honeymoon.

Don’t plan your honeymoon by yourself

 

 

A honeymoon is one of those holidays that requires input from both parties. You may enjoy a lot of the same things as a couple, but at the end of the day, you will have at least a few things you can’t agree on, and holiday destinations can certainly be one of those.

One of you may be fond of the beach while the other may enjoy the upcountry more. When you make decisions together, you can compromise and find middle ground so that you both have a great time in the end.

Don’t judge a book by its cover

That is, a picture is not always worth a thousand words - more so in photos of locations. Many promotional images are, more often than not, misleading, owing to great photoshop, filters, angles and tweaking. This leads to a lot of disappointed honeymooners. Always use reputable websites and professionals and read as many reviews as you can to get a gist of things.

Don’t bite off more than you can chew

 

It's easy to get caught up and plan a honeymoon with an intense itinerary. While this is great if that's what you both enjoy, you will eventually tire. Take it slow and leave plenty of time for relaxation. Don’t forget that you’re already probably exhausted from your wedding festivities.

Don’t forget to consider medical aspects

 

Most exotic locations usually require immunization against yellow fever other various ailments. So make sure you’re all read up on your destination and have all procedures done in time. In addition, you’ll need your usual medicines in case of emergencies such as forms of contraception, painkillers and antihistamines.
