Making The Best Of Your First 12 Months As Husband And Wife

March 28, 2017 2:49 pm

The first year of married life is a beautiful yet daunting time. A make it or break it opportunity. Consider the following  if you are committed to giving your marriage the best possible start.

You Can Do Without The Added Stress

During the first year of marriage you are already dealing with a lot of changes. Getting used to your partner, to a new home and getting used to the new responsibilities that comes with being married should take up the bulk of your emotional and physical energy during the first year. So avoid taking on additional stress that will put a strain on your relationship with your spouse during this crucial time. Things to avoid if possible include, changing jobs, taking stressful examinations, undertaking foreign travel or work assignments and in some cases conceiving and having a baby. It could be argued that challenges make a marriage stronger and it is very true that it does.

A strong foundation laid during the first year of marriage will enable a marriage to be strong enough to withstand the challenges and pressures which will inevitably come in later years.

 

Never Compare

No two people are the same and as such no two marriages are the same. Therefore never compare your marriage with that of a friend. Instead concentrate on building a marriage and a relationship with your spouse that is special and unique to you two. Be private about the details of your marriage especially if you have had a fight or argument with your spouse. Don’t be quick to judge and assume that your marriage is not as good as your friend’s based on what he/she shares with you. Taking this approach will help you see how special and wonderful your marriage is. 

 

Your Privacy And Your Space

Wherever possible try to move in to a place of your own at least for the first 12 months of marriage. Living on your own means you take on all the responsibilities that come with the territory and learn to work like a awesome husband and wife team. It means when you fight and argue you have the privacy to work out your differences without outside interference. It means you get the space to really get to know each other. But most importantly you learn to rely exclusively on each other as you take on life, making your friendship and bond even deeper.

 

In Laws And Extended Family

If it didn’t already happen during the organizing of the wedding, the first year of marriage is when you define your relationship with your in laws and extended family. In Sri Lanka quite often one does marry the family and not just the person. Every marriage benefits from the love and support of extended family in the long term and during the first year of marriage it is important that these relationships are fostered.

The trick is to be clear about boundaries. During the first year of marriage speak openly and honestly about how you would like these relationships to be maintained with your spouse. Some areas to focus on include how much of time/interaction you would like with families on a regular basis  and maintaining equal rapport with both sides of the families. Most importantly there must be an agreement between your spouse and you on decision making.

Decision that pertain yourself, your marriage and your future should be made by the two of you and no one else!

 

Discuss Finances

As a single guy or girl you may have enjoyed the luxury of spending your hard earned money, primarily on yourself. But you will soon find out that marriage is an expensive affair! You are now jointly responsible for running a fully functional house hold which means grocery and utility bills will soon eat up in to your recreational spending.

So who spends for what exactly?

Well there is no universally accepted formula here. However what is important is that you have an agreement with your spouse regarding how the financial burden of your life together will be shared. Tragically many marriages do end up in divorce way too often on account of money matters gone sour. So have this conversation with your spouse right at the outset and do not to let money matters take away the sweetness of the new life you share.

 

Define Your Own Kind Of Romance

 

In a good marriage there is always a bit of romance. A little bit of magic that keeps the marriage exciting. This special thing that keeps your marriage alive is what is popularly referred to as romance and this is really defined by each couple , mostly uniquely for themselves.

So while movies, romance novels and posts by other friends on facebook will tell you that romance involves candlelit dinners and diamond rings, the truth is that you need and your spouse will need to create your own unique brand of romance.

Make a habit of creating your own kind of romance right from the start and let the first twelve months of your life together set the bar high. If you think cooking a meal together in your tiny little kitchen every Friday night is romantic, then so be it ! And don’t forget to post the food photographs on facebook for all to see !

 

Build Your Circle Of Friends

One of the great things about being married is that you get spend as much time as you want to with your sweetheart who is now your husband or wife. But while you two get lost in your world remember that its also fun to spend some time with mutual friends.

Invite them over to your home to just hang out or go out for dinner or a weekend get away. There is nothing wrong in spending some girls only or boys only time on a regular basis, and in fact spending a little time a part is very healthy for any marriage. So during your first year of marriage, don’t neglect you friends. Let them know you are still around and that you are still the occasional social animal.  

 

Build A Home Together

As a newly married couple you are likely to move in to a space of your own. It could be a house, a tiny apartment or even a one bedroom space. Whatever the square acreage you occupy treat it like your own little piece of heaven on earth, because that’s exactly what it is. Make it a comfortable and cozy and let the floors, walls, sofas and beds be embellished with things that reflect both your personalities. Build this space together. Let it be your project during the first year of marriage. Make use of those gift vouchers you got for the wedding and shop around together for the stuff you need and want. You’ll be surprised as to how much you will learn about each other in this process.

 

Have Fun

Don’t take life too seriously. There maybe many daunting tasks to take on as you start to build your life together, but at least for the first 12 months try not to let it all overwhelm and weigh you down. Try to put them on hold and just enjoy your time together. Be a little carefree, splurge a little here and there and do things that make the two of you happy. Consider it an investment in your relationship.

If you build a strong marriage there is no challenge you cannot over come together.
