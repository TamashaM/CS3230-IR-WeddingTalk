Trendiest bridal lehenga draping styles to follow

January 25, 2018 11:29 am

Getting married? Looking for something extra for the big day?

Keep reading.

Your drape is the bling to that pretty lehenga, it can change everything. A new look, or even modernize your mum’s traditional wedding lehenga you’re carrying on.

Here are a few different styles to keep you going; 


Cowl gather dupatta drape  

Image source: onepitchcatch.com

A captivating, always in trend look for your splendid day. Simply throw the dupatta on your shoulders and pin it on to your blouse, whilst making sure that the cowl is gathered to your fit.

 

Traditional saree drape with a waist belt  

Image source: pinterest.com

A twist with the traditional saree drape, a gracefully bewitching look. Tuck in one end of the dupatta on to your skirt while twisting it around the back and bringing it to the front to form a typical saree drape. Hook on any waist belt of your choice to complete the look. 

 

Pleated dupatta with gathers on the bottom  


Image source: Pinterest.com

Tantalize your guests with this delicate drape for an alluring night. The pleated end is delicately pinned on to the blouse while forming subtle gathers at the bottom, twisting it back and around your hip, gently tucking it into the back of your skirt. Make sure to pin it in firmly to avoid any form of disasters. 

 

Dupatta left loosely over arm  

Image source: (Left) Pinterest.com (Right) Kalkifashion.com

A classy yet very radiant look for the bride looking to try something simple with a touch of swag. Diagonally throw one end of the dupatta on your preferred side, pinning one end of the horizontal side of the dupatta while allowing the other to casually fall over your arm. Remember to pin the part of the dupatta crossing over your skirt. 

 

Double dupatta style - on the head and saree style 

Image source: Frugal2fab.com

Dazzling, delightful, divine dupatta drape for an exclusive look for your bridal lehenga. Make sure to pin your dupatta draped over your head very carefully as it might be an additional weight to carry on besides that heavy gown and fancy jewellery. The second drape can be played around with based on which saree style drape you prefer the most (Gujarati style for an instance)

 

Dupatta draped over-head and thrown over arm

Image source: vjvfashion.com

An out of ordinary drape if you’re willing to attempt at something new. One end of the dupatta it tucked and guarded on to the front side of your waist then draped over your head and thrown over your arm (the same side as the first end of the tucked in dupatta). Just the thing if you’re looking to be extra noticed. 

 

The back “U” dupatta style

A grand sumptuous drape for a dupatta fashioned to be on the heavier side. The dupatta is tucked on the back of your skirt forming a “U” shape and then taken over to the front allowing it to free fall over your blouse, preferably to extend beyond your waist length. 

The Ultimate Classic One​

A very easy to do, charming drape for your lehenga. Classics are always the best, especially if you’re looking to keep it old school. Comparatively easier to handle without the hassle of an additional concern. Simply throw the dupatta over your preferred shoulder, making sure to pin it in on all the right places and you’re good to go. 

Setting it all aside, remember to smile, that being the trendiest of them all.
