4 Mistakes Sri Lankans Make When Picking Decor For Their Wedding

March 15, 2017 2:48 pm

 

Weddings are constantly evolving and metamorphosing into fantastically unique events. In spite of that, it seems like no matter how much we forge ahead, we still have a few things holding us back. Prime among those is that we are famed for finding comfort in familiarity.

So how exactly are we going wrong?

To help us out, we roped in Charith De Silva, Owner/Creative Director of MAIC - which specialises in “concept decor” - who listed 04 mistakes he believes we-Sri Lankans make when picking decor for their wedding.

 

1. Use your chosen colour scheme wisely.

Source: Pinterest.com

 

Many Sri Lankan couples are sticklers for drowning their weddings in a particular colour scheme in keeping with their theme. A sentiment Charith finds to be too common. “Often brides/couples want to have their selected colour on everything from the bridesmaid's sarees to cake boxes, to table linen, to flowers, to cake boxes, to napkins and finally focus lights with the chosen colour on to the walls as well.” This, he says, is a no-no. “Your colour doesn't have to go into each and everything at your wedding. It is always a lot more elegant and pleasing to the eye to have the chosen colour appear in patches mixed with a secondary colour like a gold, bronze or white.”

 

2. Personalise your decor.

This was custom created by maic for two personal trainers who got married. Natasha and Isuru from fit.lk. When two sexy personal trainers decided to get married, we wanted everything to be about fitness

Source: MAIC


Think decor at weddings you’ve been to are lovely but generic? Charith thinks so too. “Today, if you take the couple out of a wedding you will realise that everything else is starting to look the same. Often couples tend to borrow/copy ideas and decor from other weddings they have been to. It is your big day so why copy decor from your friend's, cousin's or neighbour's wedding?” Personalisation is very much on trend, Charith assures us, adding “Global trends for this year shows that couples are heading towards having elements in their decor that sometimes only make sense to the two of them. You have a story, use that and moments in your relationship to personalise your decor. Make it interesting and engaging.”

 

3. A Settee back is old fashioned.

Source: Pinterest.com

 

Chances are, if you have been to a Sri Lankan wedding you will undoubtedly spot a  settee back. It has become such a typical part of weddings now that it's considered a staple. After all, it makes a great backdrop to pose against. “People are of the opinion that a settee back is must for a wedding simply for photography purposes. Wedding photography is moving out of posed/made up shots and starting to look more natural and is all about capturing moments” Charith explained. “It is better to stick to just a head table which is a lot trendier and also a huge saving in terms of cost rather than spending on both a settee back and a head table”


4. Weddings are not all about flowers!

Carnival themed wedding. Not a single flower was used. Even the poruwa was custom made inspired by a merry-go-round.

Source: MAIC

 

You would be hard pressed to find a Sri Lankan wedding that didn’t have flowers in it. Flowers have been unequivocally voted “Be creative, explore options without flowers for your decor, you will be surprised with the outcome. Though traditionally weddings are made of flowers and flowers only, its trending now to explore 'flowerless' options and pushing boundaries. This will also give you the freedom to be more creative and come up with something that is different and decor that will stand out from what's commonly seen around weddings in Colombo.”
