Tips on having a Stress Free Wedding

January 29, 2018 2:19 pm

Start with Finances

Image source: mrspandp.com

When planning a wedding a lot of the decisions you need to make are based on how much money you have in hand to spend. For example, your top pick for floral décor might be exorbitant and you might discover this too late in the planning cycle resulting in having to rework and revise plans you have already made for attire, the music and catering.

You may for an example assume that your fiancé will be paying for the photography, only to find out later that he isn’t. What can be more stressful than that? 

Therefore start your wedding plan by setting your overall wedding budget and getting over the tricky business of finalizing who pays for what. This may involve chats regarding funding with your parents, your to-be spouse and even an assessment to your own finances.

With your wedding budget in hand, you now have clarity on which type of vendors you will engage with and what type of wedding you can afford to host. 


Be the super organized bride 

It’s time to invest in a dedicated wedding journal and some files and folders! As you go deeper into wedding planning you will find that those wedding vendor brochures, samples, supplier quotations, receipts, bills etc do pile up pretty fast.

Your to-do list will expand with every passing day and to be on top of this complex web of information is daunting and stressful. Therefore, don’t underestimate the importance of being organized.

Most brides rely on a master to-do list and a wedding budget which can be easily managed and manipulated on a Microsoft excel sheet. In addition, a wedding journal or notebook is needed for scribbling down of wedding-related information. Meticulously documenting and filing all things wedding related is important for easy reference at any time. 


Delegate. Delegate. Delegate

While its natural to want to be in control of every single detail of your wedding, it is certainly not practical especially if you are planning a wedding with a full time job.

Enlist friends and family in the project and delegate tasks to them. Share your expectations and the wedding vision with them, trust them to complete the tasks for you and allow them to take on the tasks independently but in consultation with you.

This allows you to fully focus on the aspects of the wedding which are of absolute importance to you as a bride such as your attire and makeup perhaps, which are usually things you cannot delegate effectively.

Groom a wedding lieutenant

It always helps to have one person out there who knows as much as you about your wedding. This person can be a friend who is not a part of your retinue or even a professional like a wedding coordinator.

The idea is to take the stress of carrying the entire burden of knowing all the wedding-related nitty gritty off of your shoulders and having the comfort of knowing that without your direct involvement the wedding work will carry on.

You will find this useful especially on the big day when you are unlikely to be in a position to do anything except get dressed and get married and attending to the even the tiniest of tasks or answering even the smallest wedding related query will be very stressful.

Engage professionals for the job 

Image source: startupguys.net

While you may have a friend who can do nice cake structures and another workmate who is pretty good at flower arrangements it is always best to hand over wedding-related work to professionals who have been tried and tested.

There are wedding related suppliers to suit almost every budget and price range out there in the market so even on a tight budget there is no reason not to engage professionals to get the job done.

Professionals are experienced and skilled specialists in their area and are especially good at handling unexpected emergencies. With professionals on the job, your mind will be at ease at all times. 

Ditch the Bridezilla mentality

Wedding day stress is a state of mind, often resulting from being too obsessive about minor details and being too demanding of one’s self and others around you. While you plan your wedding remember the big day is after all just one day in your forever after and that perfecting each detail of your wedding day is not the main aim of it all.

You can certainly aspire to create the wedding day you dreamt of but it is important to understand the limitations with which you are working with and to keep a cool head. Also, remember that no one loves a Bridezilla.
