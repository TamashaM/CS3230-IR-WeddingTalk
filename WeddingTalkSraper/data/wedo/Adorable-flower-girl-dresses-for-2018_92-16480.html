Adorable flower girl dresses for 2018

February 06, 2018 11:30 am

Selecting the perfect flower girl dress is of paramount importance as they are responsible in making your wedding retinue look extra charming and adorable.
 
The flower girl may be the smallest yet make the biggest impact as she will lead you down the aisle giving you the dream entrance you want to remember.
 
Brides are spoilt with choices to dress their little sidekicks to add to the sweet appeal. Below are a few trending styles for 2018.
 
 
Traditional Dresses
 
Nothing is more unique and prouder than that of wearing the home traditional garb. A bride wearing Kandyan (Osari) for her wedding will opt for half saree or even be bold enough to design a mini saree for her flower girls.
Image source: Studio3000df
 
Those wearing the Indian style may opt for lehengas or mini shalwars. Either way, it will certainly be enthralling especially for your foreign guests at the wedding! 
 
 
Tulle or Lace 
 
Nothing speaks elegance and childhood than a flower girl adorned in lace or tulle. With the current trend of using white on flower girl dresses to match the bride, the tulle or lace can be designed in different colours to match the wedding theme.
 
Image source: (Left) Pinterest (Right) Etsy.com
 
Besides, there are plenty of designs to choose from! Consult a bridal seamstress to see what design suits your taste and budget.
 
 
Satin and Sash 
 
This style is perhaps one of the more ubiquitous styles for couples opting for a more Western-styled attire.
 
“Many of my clients opt for a sash or bow on a satin dress. This is economical compared to that of using tulle or lace as most of my customers are middle-income folks.” says the owner at Thisara Bridal and Beauty Salon, Mrs. Chandani Ariyasena in Karawanella, a small town 16km from Avisawella.
Image source: Pinterest.com
 
 
Layered  
 
For older flower girls or those who opt for a more sophisticated, chic wedding having a layered dress adds quite a bit of subtlety to the bridal retinue.
 
This is mostly done on silk or chiffon in tulle and floral lace designs, sequin-studded or frills. You can also have a bit of fun and add glitter to make your flower girl feel like a princess!  However one must be careful not to make the dress too cumbersome or heavy or you may have to deal with one fussy flower girl! 
Image source: (Left) Pinterest (Right) dhgate.com
 
 
Bohemian/ Boho
 
With brides opting for a more vintage looks at their wedding, one of the trendier styles is the boho look. Silk, chiffon and linen adorned with lace or ribbon are the most opted fabrics for this look.
 
Boho look radiates the childish glow in the wearer and accessorizing with nature-inspired flower crowns will certainly make them look more glamorous radiating positive vibes to one and all! 
 
Image source: Pinterest.com
 
While you may have these trendy styles to select from it is also important to keep in mind the colour, fabric, pattern (dress, neckline, and sleeve), length and accessories that go along with the style you have chosen.
 
One of leading bridal dressing owners in Sri Lanka Mr. Dhananjaya Bandara mused that couples now go for more subtle, nude and soft colours for their flower girls rather than bright and flashy colours.
 
Pearl whites and white shades are paired with colours like purple to contrast the waist sash, shoes and other accessories. The most opted for length is ankle level. While his clients do not hesitate to spend on a quality design and fabric there is lesser preferences to having a large number of flower girls in the retinue.
 
It is also important to note that sometimes, the flower girl’s dress has an equivalent fabric amount to that of the bridesmaids as the dress may be in layers. As a designer, he prefers to use as less makeup and jewellery as possible onto the flower girl to let her young fresh look radiate itself. 
 
Therefore as much as you, as the bride would love the little ones to look good it is most important to ensure she feels comfortable throughout the day so that you and the young ones will have that brightest smile on all wedding photos which you can reminisce and cherish for the years to come!
