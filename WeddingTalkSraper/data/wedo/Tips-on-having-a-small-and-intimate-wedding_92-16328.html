Tips on having a small and intimate wedding

November 07, 2017 11:20 am

When it comes to weddings, we're bound to think the traditional way. We are used to the large crowds, big celebrations, unfamiliar faces and endless details which can often stress us out!

 

Such are the weddings we’ve witnessed from our parents’ time and it's not really necessary for you to follow the same route.

As times have changed, more and more couples are considering the idea of a small and intimate wedding ceremony. Small and intimate weddings have many advantages.

Having only those closest to you attend your special day makes it a more personal celebration and allows you to spend quality time with all your loved ones throughout the day while making your wedding day less stressful and allowing you to focus on what really matters – loving and committing to your partner.

If you have decided to have a small and intimate wedding, here are some tips that will help you plan out your special day!

 

Make the most of your budget

 

Smaller weddings don’t necessarily have to be simple affairs. With a limited guest list, you can put your financial resources to better use.

Do something unique.

Make the most of your wedding budget by splurging on your priorities. This could be a fantastic meal, great wedding favours, or excellent entertainment for your guests.   
 
 

Limit the guest list

This may seem like a no-brainer, but keeping your guest list small is an important part of planning an intimate wedding. While cutting down extended family members and friends is tough, paring down your guest list will be worth it.

You will feel all the more special when you look around and realise you are surrounded by the most important people in your life on your wedding day.

There will definitely be a protest from both families when you say you want to cut down on their guests. There’s no easy way to do this except to be perfectly honest.

Explain to your extended families and friends that you’re keeping the event very small and hence the reason for limiting the list, but be prepared for the occasional hurtful feelings amongst family members. 

 

Choose your venue wisely

 

When you have a smaller guest list, your venue options are aplenty. Either choose a location that is meaningful to you or find a venue that is flexible with the guaranteed numbers.

You can choose your favourite restaurant, a grand hotel, a picturesque park or a beautiful venue with exquisite scenery to make your wedding extra special. 

 

Involve the guests

You will have plenty of time to meet and greet each and every one of your guests when there are fewer people on the list. Your guests will appreciate you more when you devote special attention to them.

Plan the wedding day in such a way that they can all be involved in the wedding celebration. For example, seating your guests in horseshoe type tables or long group tables or a mix of both works great!

All guests together in one table creates a more intimate setting and allows everyone with one another rather than from across the room. 

 

Make it entertaining

The entertainment you choose to incorporate into your wedding is usually based on what type of atmosphere you want to create. Larger wedding functions usually involve a party with a DJ, but for smaller weddings, something like an elegant dinner with live music whether it is a saxophonist, piano or guitarist who adds a lovely touch, would be preferred.

You could also opt for background music or a sound system to play your own choice of music.

 

Make it a destination wedding

Use the small number of your nearest and dearest to your advantage. Take over an Inn out in the mountains or book a few rooms at some of Sri Lanka's splendid hotels down south giving yourself the opportunity for a beach wedding.   

A destination wedding offers couples, their families, and their friends the once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to travel together and spend an exotic weekend away relaxing and brunching while enjoying the festivities!
